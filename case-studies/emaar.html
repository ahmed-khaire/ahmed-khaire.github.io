Emaar Properties — CRM, Broker Portal & Inventory

Designing and delivering the Salesforce CRM, broker management portal, and real-time inventory system powering property sales across Dubai Hills Estate, Creek Harbour, and Emaar's other flagship communities.

The Client

Emaar Properties is the UAE's largest real estate developer — the company behind Burj Khalifa, Dubai Mall, Dubai Hills Estate, and Dubai Creek Harbour. At this scale, property sales run through thousands of brokers, tens of thousands of units, and launch events where entire towers sell in hours. The systems behind that process have to be real-time, reliable, and effortless for brokers to use.

This engagement was delivered during Ahmed's tenure as Software Solution Architect at PwC.

The Challenge

Sales at flagship-launch scale exposes every weakness in a CRM stack: brokers need instant, accurate unit availability; sales teams need a single view of every lead and booking; and finance needs payment milestones tracked without manual reconciliation. Fragmented tooling means double-booked units, missed follow-ups, and brokers working from stale spreadsheets during the busiest hours of a launch.

What Was Delivered

  • Salesforce CRM covering the end-to-end sales lifecycle — lead capture, opportunity management, bookings, and customer handovers.
  • Broker management portal — self-service onboarding and deal submission for Emaar's broker network, giving agencies direct, governed access to the sales process.
  • Real-time inventory system — unit availability, pricing, and holds synchronized across sales channels, built to stay accurate under launch-day load.
  • Enterprise integrations connecting Salesforce with ERP, payment, and document systems so bookings flow through to finance without re-keying.

Architecture Highlights

Scale-first design

Data models and sharing architecture designed for organizations with hundreds of internal users plus an external broker network.

Event-driven integration

Platform Events and API-led integration patterns keeping inventory, bookings, and finance systems consistent in real time.

Governed broker access

Security and visibility architecture giving external agencies exactly the data they need — and nothing more.

Stakeholder alignment

Discovery workshops and solution design documents translating C-level goals into a technical roadmap delivery teams could execute.
