Majid Al Futtaim — Enterprise CRM & CPQ Across 3 Countries

An enterprise-wide Salesforce implementation — end-to-end Sales Cloud and CPQ for the property sales lifecycle across all Majid Al Futtaim properties in the UAE, Oman, and Egypt, including Tilal Al Ghaf and City Centre communities.

The Client

Majid Al Futtaim operates across the Middle East and North Africa — from Mall of the Emirates to master-planned communities like Tilal Al Ghaf. Its property business spans multiple countries, currencies, legal frameworks, and sales processes, all of which needed to run on one CRM platform without forcing every market into an identical mold.

This engagement was delivered during Ahmed's tenure as Software Solution Architect at PwC.

The Challenge

Multi-country CRM rollouts fail in one of two ways: either every market builds its own disconnected variant, or headquarters imposes a rigid single process nobody adopts. MAF needed a shared core — one data model, one pricing engine, one reporting layer — flexible enough to absorb per-country differences in payment plans, taxes, and sales workflows.

What Was Delivered

  • End-to-end Sales Cloud for the full property sales lifecycle — enquiry, reservation, booking, and contract — deployed across the UAE, Oman, and Egypt.
  • CPQ (Revenue Cloud) handling unit pricing, payment plan configuration, promotions, and quote generation — replacing spreadsheet-based pricing with a governed engine.
  • Multi-country architecture — a shared core with market-specific extensions for currencies, taxation, and localized sales processes.
  • Cross-functional delivery — coordinating developers, consultants, and stakeholders across markets through shared design documents, ERDs, and governance.

Architecture Highlights

Shared core, local flex

One data model and pricing engine with per-market configuration — avoiding both fragmentation and forced uniformity.

Governed pricing

CPQ rules encoding payment plans and promotions so every quote is accurate, approved, and auditable.

Regional reporting

Consolidated pipeline and sales reporting across countries for group-level visibility.

Delivery governance

Design authority, code review standards, and CI/CD practices keeping concurrent country workstreams consistent.
